Poppy Ergo Jr is a low cost arm designed for education. It is easy to build and to modify. Thanks to visual programation it can be used in school to realize from small to more complex projects.

Features

Originally designed in the "Mathematics a beautiful elsewhere" artistic experiment, the Ergo Jr has been adapted to fit with education needs.

It is a robotic arm, made of 6 motors allowing for life-like movements. The pieces are 3D printed. Thanks to the use of rivets they can be assembled, modified, and reassembled with ease. Ergo Jr comes with three tools - a lampshade, a gripper and a pen holder - permitting the realisation of various projects.

We use a Raspberry-Pi board for control and add a camera so the robot can interact with the world.

We promote an educative approach and an interactive technology. We let everything transparent and accessible.

Program your robot

Ergo Jr relies on Snap! a variant of scratch the visual programming language. Its accessibility and efficiency make it a great tool to discover robotics and understand the digital world. You can use it directly on the robot and do not need to install anything.
